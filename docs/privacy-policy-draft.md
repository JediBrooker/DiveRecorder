## 2. What DivingHQ Does

DivingHQ is a diving competition platform. It helps organisations register users, manage clubs and teams, create competitions, collect dive lists, assign officials, run live judging, publish public scoreboards, export programs and results, send notifications, and provide athlete, coach, and judge analytics.

Because competition results are inherently public in many sporting contexts, some information such as athlete names, clubs, countries, events, scores, rankings, dive lists, and public scoreboard data may be visible to spectators and included in result archives.

## 3. Personal Information We Collect

We collect personal information directly from users, from organisation administrators, from meet managers, from coaches, from judges and referees, from competition activity, and automatically from devices and browsers.

### Account and identity information

This may include username, full name, email address, password hash, organisation, country or federation, club, team, roles, role requests, approval status, public profile slug, email verification status, two-factor authentication status, and account settings.

We do not store readable passwords or readable two-factor recovery codes.

### Competition information

This may include event entries, age group or division, gender category, board height, dive lists, synchro partner or team membership, event attendance or check-in status, withdrawals, substitutions, reserve status, rankings, scores, dive-off records, score corrections, and meet results.

### Coach, judge, referee, and official information

This may include judging assignments, panel position, role approvals, coach-diver links, referee sign-off activity, score submissions, score-correction reasons, judge analytics, dashboard preferences, and operational actions taken during a meet.

### Public scoreboard and archive information

For live or completed events, public pages may show names, clubs, countries, teams, dive codes, degree of difficulty, per-dive scores, rankings, result summaries, meet schedule information, programs, start lists, score sheets, PDFs, CSV exports, and other information normally associated with a sporting competition result.

We do not intentionally publish account email addresses, password information, private notes, two-factor authentication secrets, or push-subscription keys.

### Device, log, and security information

We may collect IP address, approximate location inferred from IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, user agent, timestamps, pages or endpoints accessed, socket connection activity, authentication attempts, rate-limit events, audit-log events, and other technical data needed to operate and secure the service.

### Notifications and communications

We may collect email delivery information, password reset and email verification activity, notification preferences, browser push subscription endpoints and keys, notification delivery status, support messages, and administrative correspondence.

### Cookies, local storage, and similar technologies

DivingHQ may use browser storage, IndexedDB, service workers, authentication tokens, local preferences, language settings, and similar technologies to keep users signed in, cache app data, support offline or faster loading, remember settings, and provide security. If we add analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking in the future, we will update this policy and, where required, request consent.

### Information we do not intentionally collect

DivingHQ is not designed to collect medical records, biometric identifiers, government identity numbers, payment card numbers, or highly sensitive personal information. Users and organisations should not enter sensitive information into free-text fields unless it is necessary and they have authority to provide it.

## 10. Retention

We keep personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer period is required or permitted by law.

Typical retention periods:

- Account records: for as long as the account is active, and for a reasonable period after closure where needed for security, legal, dispute, backup, or competition-integrity reasons.
- Public competition results and archives: retained as historical sporting records unless removal or redaction is required or appropriate.
- Dive lists, scores, rankings, and event records: retained to support result history, analytics, record keeping, exports, and dispute handling.
- Security and audit logs: generally retained for a limited operational period. DivingHQ currently purges core audit logs from the production database after approximately 30 days, unless audit snapshots or backups are configured for legal-retention purposes.
- Password reset and verification tokens: retained only for the life of the verification or reset process, then expired or replaced.
- Push subscriptions: retained until revoked, expired, or no longer needed.
- Backups: retained according to backup schedules and then overwritten or deleted.

Deletion requests may not remove public result records, audit records, backup records, or records we must keep for legal, security, fraud-prevention, competition-integrity, or dispute-resolution purposes. Where full deletion is not possible, we may restrict, redact, de-identify, or minimise information where appropriate.

## 11. Security

We use technical and organisational safeguards designed to protect personal information, including HTTPS, password hashing, authentication controls, role-based access controls, optional two-factor authentication, audit logging, rate limiting, security headers, database access controls, and operational monitoring.

No online service can guarantee absolute security. Users are responsible for keeping passwords confidential, using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication where available, and telling us promptly about suspected unauthorised access.

## 12. Children and Minors

Diving is a youth sport, and DivingHQ may process information about minors when a club, federation, school, meet host, parent, guardian, coach, or authorised adult uses the service for a competition.

DivingHQ is not intended for unsupervised use by children under the age at which they can lawfully consent to online services in their country. In many places this is under 13, under 16, or another locally defined age. Users under the applicable age must use DivingHQ only with permission from a parent or guardian, or through an authorised sporting organisation that has obtained any required consents.

For users in the United States, we do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent or another legally permitted basis. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information without required consent, contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

For users in Australia, consent for a person under 18 may depend on maturity and capacity. Where capacity is unclear, parent or guardian consent may be required.

Parents, guardians, and authorised organisations may contact us to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, or review of a minor's personal information, subject to applicable law and competition-record requirements.
